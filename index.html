Biography

Md. Kawsher Mahbub is a computer science educator and researcher with a strong passion for advancing education and technology. He earned his Master’s degree in Computer Science (CGPA 3.80) from Kent State University, USA, where he also served as a Graduate Teaching Assistant, delivering lectures, conducting labs, and mentoring undergraduate students. Prior to that, he worked as a Lecturer at Bangladesh University of Business and Technology (BUBT), teaching core computer science courses and supervising undergraduate projects. Kawsher’s research interests lie in Artificial Intelligence, Health Informatics, Deep Learning, and Computer Vision. He has published 11 peer-reviewed papers in respected international journals and conferences, including Information Sciences, Frontiers in Genetics, and Expert Systems with Applications. His research contributions focus on medical image analysis, explainable AI, and machine learning approaches for disease detection, demonstrating his commitment to impactful, real-world problem solving. Beyond research and teaching, Kawsher is an active peer reviewer for IEEE CBMS (2023, 2024) and CMC – Computers, Materials & Continua (Tech Science Press). He also engages in leadership and community service, serving as Secretary of the Bangladesh Student Association at Kent State University and advising student groups to promote collaboration and technical skill development. Kawsher aspires to inspire the next generation of computer scientists through student-centered teaching, cutting-edge research, and innovative problem solving, with the goal of contributing to global advancements in AI for healthcare and education.
